Somatoform Disorders in Psychiatry

Somatoform disorders are psychiatric conditions where individuals experience distressing physical symptoms that cannot be fully explained by medical illness, and are instead linked to psychological factors. In DSM-5, these are grouped under Somatic Symptom and Related Disorders.

Key Types

  • Somatization Disorder: Multiple recurrent physical complaints without adequate medical explanation, beginning before age 30 and persisting for years.
  • Hypochondriasis (Illness Anxiety Disorder): Persistent fear of having a serious illness despite medical reassurance.
  • Conversion Disorder: Neurological symptoms (paralysis, blindness, seizures) inconsistent with medical findings, often linked to psychological stress.
  • Pain Disorder: Chronic pain with significant psychological factors contributing to its severity or persistence.
  • Body Dysmorphic Disorder: Preoccupation with imagined or exaggerated defects in physical appearance.
  • Somatic Symptom Disorder: Excessive thoughts, feelings, or behaviors related to distressing physical symptoms, regardless of whether a medical condition is present.

Clinical Importance

  • High prevalence in primary care and psychiatry.
  • Functional impairment with repeated medical visits and disability.
  • Frequently associated with anxiety and depression.
  • Management includes psychoeducation, CBT, stress management, and sometimes SSRIs/SNRIs.
